# NYC Reference List — Ridgewood Skill

This file is the ground truth for the Ridgewood skill. When evaluating output quality for any city, compare against these venues and neighborhoods. If a recommended venue feels inconsistent with this list, it likely doesn't meet the criteria.

## Reference Neighborhoods (in order)

| Rank | Neighborhood | Gentrification Stage | Key signals |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Ridgewood, Queens | In progress | Transit-isolated, Eastern European/Latino community, hyper-local press (Bushwick Daily), StreetEasy #1 two years running |
| 2 | Red Hook, Brooklyn | Slow/organic | No subway (bus + ferry only), village feel, artisan community, Time Out #1 coolest NYC 2025, hyper-local press (Star-Revue) |
| 3 | Bushwick, Brooklyn | Recent (5–10 years) | Dense creative/arts scene, strong independent venue mix, industrial feel |
| 4 | Williamsburg, Brooklyn | Advanced but valid | Venue diversity maintained; avoid north near Bedford Ave for tourist traps |
| 5 | Lower East Side, Manhattan | Advanced but valid | Historic immigrant identity visible, dive bar culture intact; avoid tourist corridors |

## What this list tells us

When validating recommendations for other cities, ask:
- Does this venue feel like it would fit into one of these neighborhoods?
- Would a regular at Duck Duck or Clandestino feel at home here?
- Is the clientele mix similar — locals, workers, artists, long-term residents?
- Does the owner/staff have visible presence and identity in the venue?

If the answer is yes, the venue is likely a good fit.
